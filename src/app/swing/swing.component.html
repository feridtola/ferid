Swing Trading,

is a trading methodology that seeks to capture a swing (or “one move”) in the markets. The idea is to endure as “little pain” as possible by exiting your trades before the opposing pressure comes in. This means you’ll book your profits before the market reverse and wipe out your gains. Swing trading positions are typically held for a few days to several weeks, but can be held for longer periods of time. Swing trading is one of the few trading approaches that’s suitable for the retail trader

Strategy 1: Stuck in a box

Stuck in a Box identifies a range market and waits for the price to drop below support. You want to take profits before the resistance level is reached, which increases the likelihood of a successful trade.

    That´s how it works:

  • Identify a range market.
  • Wait until the price is below the support.
  • If the price is below support, then wait for a strong price rejection (a close above Support)
  • If there’s a strong price rejection, then go long on the next candle open
  • Set a stop loss on an avarage true range (ATR) below the candlestick low and take profits before Resistance.
Strategy 2: Catch the Wave

The aim of this strategy is to capture the "one move" in trending markets (like a surfer trying to catch the wave). If a surfer wants to ride a wave that allows them to do great tricks, they need to catch the right one and know when to move. The idea here is to enter after a pullback has ended. Once the trend changes, the trend towards greater investment withdrawal brings greater opportunities for profit. It is recommended to look for trends with 50 MA (moving average).

    Now, let’s see how to catch the wave with this strategy:

  • If the market approaches the moving average, then wait for a bullish price rejection.
  • If there’s a bullish price rejection, then go long on next candle.
  • Set your stop loss 1 ATR below the low and take profits just before the swing high.
Strategy 3: Fade the Move

With this strategy, a trader will sell and expect the momentum to weaken in an uptrend. Basically, this strategy acts against the trend (also known as counter-trend).

    That´s how it works:

  • Identify a range market.
  • Identify a strong momentum move into Resistance that takes out the previous high.
  • Look for a strong price rejection as the candle forms a strong bearish close
  • Go short on the next candle and set your stop loss 1 ATR above the highs
  • Take profits before the nearest swing low.
